About Me

Hi, I’m Miguel Mandelli, a Brazilian guitarist based in Germany.

My work reaches the classical guitar repertoire with a new perspective, as well as new arrangements for solo guitar and South American repertoire. I work with both traditional single top guitars and double top guitars, depending on the repertoire or concert hall. On this website you will find all information regarding concerts, recorded albums, videos, biography and my contact.

Make sure to visit social media for more information.

Biography

I was born in 1995 in Criciúma, Brazil, into a family of musicians. After learning many instruments, I began playing the guitar at the age of 15 and won my first competitions the following year. I started my studies at the Tatuí Music Conservatory in São Paulo, Brazil, under the guidance of Angela Muner, a well-known Brazilian guitarist who taught me a lot in the beginning. In addition to my solo guitar studies, I also worked intensively on chamber music, with many different formations (duos, trios, quartets) and styles. I was also the first guitarist in the chamber guitar orchestra, conducted by Edson Lopes, which also taught me a lot about chamber music.

After my studies in Brazil, I decided to explore the world of guitar and its different schools. I lived in Ireland, where I had the opportunity to play many concerts throughout the year, as well as in Spain, Italy, and Austria, where I could learn from Petra Poláčková, a unique guitarist and teacher with a worldwide career. However, it was in 2018 when I decided to study in Germany, at the Hochschule für Musik und Tanz Köln, under the guidance of the globally renowned Professor and soloist Hubert Käppel, where I am still studying and delving deeper.

Nowadays I work as an active soloist, chamber musician, and teacher, playing in Brazil and many countries in Europe, such as England, Italy, Germany, Ireland, Croatia, and also participating in festivals as an artist and jury of competitions such as Valle-Bale international guitar competition in Croatia, and Enrico Mercatalli Altamira international guitar competition in Gorizia, Italy.

Throughout my journey, I have always been actively involved in various festivals, where I had the opportunity to learn from different renowned classical guitarists such as Fabio Zanon, Aniello Desiderio, Paolo Pegoraro, David Russell, Marcelo Kayath, Sergio Abreu, Rafael Aguirre, Ana Vidovic, Sergio Assad, Odair Assad, Goran Krivokapic, Tillman Hoppstock, Carlo Marchione, Göran Sollscher, Lukasz Kuropaczewski, Gérard Abiton, Judicael Perroy, Zoran Dukić, and others.

In addition to all that, I have always invested a lot in the art of teaching, striving to make all the information I have gathered along my journey accessible to younger generations. Currently, I teach at the Rheinische Musikschule Köln, the State Music School Sankt Augustin, where I also coordinate the department of plucked strings, and at the Ida Bieler Music Academy in Köln.
